Rules and Information : Ludi

LUDI is a game-generating computer program that implements Cameron Browne's algorithm for generating new games.

LUDI was developed by Cameron Browne as part of his PhD in AI and game design



This LUDI software system:


  • defines the scope
  • defines the rules of the game
  • interprets the game and coordinates a testplays
  • measures the game quality

Introduction to Yavalath

Rules and Information

Yavalath is the most successful game evolved by LUDI in 2007 and published in 2009 by Nestorgames (Spain).



awarded with :


  • Dean's Award for Outstanding Thesis
  • Yavalath won 2012 the Humies Award for Human-Competitive Result Produced by Genetic and Evolutionary Computation
  • It was also rated onto place 8 out of 200 abstract games invented in 2007.

Basic Rules


  • 2-3 players
  • win with 4 and lose with 3 in a row
  • turnbased until :
    • one player has won
    • all but one player have lost
    • all cells are filled and it is a draw

Artificial Intelligence

UCT AI : Motivation
  • UCT has its strengths in games where:
    • the state of a game is hard to evaluate
    • the game has a high branching factor

  • Yavalath's properties suit UCT, because
    • it has no developed game theory
    • there are $3,27 * 10^{17}$ different move combinations only for the first 10 moves

Artificial Intelligence

UCT AI : UCB1 Policy
  • initialization: Play every node once
  • loop: Play node that maximizes
$\bar{x}_i+C*\sqrt{\frac{ln_n}{n_i}}$
  • UCB1 Policy:
    • $X_i$ – mean reward of node i (here: win chance)
    • $N_i$ – number of visits of node i
    • $N$ – number of total iterations
    • $C$ – adjustable constant
